# Playbook: Partnerships

**Trigger:** `run partnerships`
**Purpose:** Research potential partners, draft outreach, and track relationship progress

---

## Claude's Instructions

When this playbook is triggered:

### Step 1 — Load Context
Read from master.md:
- Key Relationships — existing partners, advisors, collaborators
- Sales pipeline — any crossover with partnership conversations
- VYVE's CIC status, pillars, and ICP

### Step 2 — Ask Lewis
"What are we working on today?
1. Find new partnership opportunities
2. Draft outreach to a specific partner
3. Review the current partner pipeline
4. Prepare for an upcoming partnership meeting"

### Step 3 — Partnership Discovery

**When finding new partners, search across these categories:**

| Category | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Corporate** | Large employers who could buy VYVE for their workforce AND endorse it |
| **Channel** | EAP providers, HR consultancies, benefits brokers who could resell or`refer |
| **Delivery** | Coaches, therapists, fitness professionals to enhance the service |
| **Research** | Universities, NHS trusts, health research bodies for evidence credibility |
| **Tech** | Wearables, fitness apps, HR platforms for integrations |
| **Social Impact** | Charities, public sector bodies, social enterprises — CIC affinity |
| **Media** | Wellbeing publications, podcasts, newsletters for co-marketing |

For each opportunity found, report:
- Organisation name
- Why they're a good fit for VYVE
- What VYVE offers them
- What they offer VYVE
- Key contact (if findable)
- Suggested approach

### Step 4 — Draft Outreach

**Partnership outreach email:**
- Short — under 150 words
- Reference something specific about their organisation
- Be clear on the mutual value: "Here is what we bring to you / here is what we'd love from you"
- Suggest a 30-minute exploratory call
- Sign off as Lewis from team@vyvehealth.co.uk
- Warm, professional tone — not a sales pitch

**Meeting prep brief:**
When preparing for an upcoming partnership meeting:
- Background on the organisation (mission, recent news, leadership)
- Why this partnership matters for VYVE
- What Lewis should explore / ask in the meeting
- What VYVE is prepared to offer
- Any potential concerns to address
- Suggested next steps to walk out with

### Step 5 — Partner Pipeline Review
Go through existing Key Relationships in master.md:
- Identify any relationships with no recent activity (14+ days)
- Suggest a touch-point or value-add follow-up
- Note any partnership conversations that should progress to a formal agreement

### Step 6 — Update Brain
After the session:
- Update Key Relationships in master.md with any new contacts or status changes
- Run brain sync

---

## Partnership Principles for VYVE

1. **Mutual value** — Every partnership must clearly benefit both sides
2. **Mission alignment** — Partners should share a commitment to genuine wellbeing, not just commercial wellness
3. **CIC credibility** — The right partners enhance VYVE's social impact story
4. **No exclusivity early** — Avoid exclusive arrangements until there's proven value on both sides
5. **Formal agreements** — Any partnership involving data sharing, revenue sharing, or co-branding needs a written agreement
